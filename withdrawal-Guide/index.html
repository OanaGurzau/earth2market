A short and comprehensive guide to Two-Factor Authentication


Download Google Authenticator from Google Play or Google Authenticator from the App Store or any other authenticator app. Google is probably the best one.

Go to Settings (in the scroll-down menu on your profile, on the right).

Scroll down to "OPEN 2FA PREFERENCES" (yes, it reads PREFENCES but technically 2FA is a fence for your account...isn't it? Jk, it will be fixed.) Click on the button.

Press Enable Two-factor authentication, then Next.

You will see a QR Code appearing, open the Google Authenticator app and press the + button on the bottom right. Now frame the QR Code with the camera, you should get a code which expires every 30 seconds. Insert the code in the browser where it is requested.

After you've done this, you will be able to show backup codes. These codes are very useful in case you lose your phone or authenticator app access, so KEEP them safe, an offline storage might be ideal (piece of paper or external drive not connected to the internet). DO NOT SHARE THESE CODES WITH ANYONE! If any of you are getting a 403 error, follow this link: CSRF Error
